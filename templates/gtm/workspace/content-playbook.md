# Content Playbook

## Content pillars

1. **Net terms education** — What are net terms, why do B2B buyers expect them, how to offer them without risk
2. **AR automation** — DSO reduction, collections automation, credit decisioning, the cost of manual AR
3. **Industry deep dives** — How net terms work in construction, manufacturing, distribution, wholesale
4. **Platform/embedded finance** — How marketplaces and platforms can offer B2B payment terms
5. **Customer stories** — Before/after: what changed when they switched from manual AR to Balance

## Content types & prompts for OpenClaw

### Blog posts
- "Draft a blog outline: Why construction companies lose $X per year on manual AR"
- "Write a blog intro about DSO trends in manufacturing"
- "Create a listicle: 5 signs your AR process is costing you money"

### Email sequences
- "Write a 3-email outbound sequence for CFOs at distribution companies"
- "Draft a nurture email about our latest case study"
- "Write a re-engagement email for prospects who went dark"

### Social posts (LinkedIn)
- "Write 3 LinkedIn posts about B2B payment trends for our company page"
- "Draft a LinkedIn post announcing [partner/feature/milestone]"
- "Create a poll about AR pain points for B2B finance leaders"

### Newsletter
- "Draft this week's newsletter section on [topic]"
- "Summarize these 3 industry articles into newsletter blurbs"
- "Write a newsletter intro tying [current event] to B2B payments"

### Sales enablement
- "Create a one-pager for [vertical/persona]"
- "Write talk track for when a prospect says 'we already use QuickBooks for AR'"
- "Draft a follow-up email after a demo with [company]"

## Voice reminders

- Lead with the prospect's pain, not our features
- Use specific numbers: "$X in bad debt", "45+ day DSO", "3 FTEs on collections"
- Speak the language of finance: DSO, aging reports, credit limits, terms (net-30/60/90)
- Don't say "seamless", "leverage", "cutting-edge" — just say what it does
